Number Systems & Order of Operations

Before tackling algebra or calculus, you must master how numbers are classified and how to evaluate expressions correctly. These are the absolute foundations every other concept builds upon.

Types of Numbers

Numbers are organised in sets, each a superset of the one before it: Natural ⊂ Whole ⊂ Integer ⊂ Rational ⊂ Real. Understanding which set a number belongs to tells you what operations are valid.

Watch: Algebra Introduction – Video-Tutor.net
NUMBER SETS
Natural (ℕ)
\(1, 2, 3, 4, \ldots\)
Whole (W)
\(0, 1, 2, 3, \ldots\)
Integers (ℤ)
\(\ldots, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, \ldots\)
Rational (ℚ)
\(\frac{p}{q},\; q \neq 0,\; p,q \in \mathbb{Z}\)
Irrational
\(\sqrt{2}, \pi, e\) (non-terminating, non-repeating)
Real (ℝ)
Rational ∪ Irrational
1
Classify each number into ALL applicable sets
Classify: \(-7,\quad \dfrac{3}{4},\quad \sqrt{5},\quad 0,\quad \sqrt{9}\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
\(-7\): Negative whole number → Integer, Rational, Real (not Natural, not Whole)
2
\(\frac{3}{4}\): Fraction of two integers, \(q \neq 0\) → Rational, Real only
3
\(\sqrt{5} \approx 2.236...\): Non-terminating, non-repeating → Irrational, Real only
4
\(0\): Zero → Whole, Integer, Rational (\(0=\frac{0}{1}\)), Real
5
\(\sqrt{9} = 3\): Perfect square, equals 3 → Natural, Whole, Integer, Rational, Real
Answer: √9 belongs to ALL five sets; √5 belongs to Irrational & Real only
2
True or False reasoning
State True or False and justify: (a) Every integer is rational. (b) Every rational is an integer. (c) \(\pi\) is real but not rational.
SOLUTION
a
True. Any integer \(n\) can be written as \(\frac{n}{1}\), which is rational.
b
False. \(\frac{1}{2}\) is rational but not an integer (no whole-number form).
c
True. \(\pi \approx 3.14159...\) never repeats or terminates → Irrational ⊂ Real.
Answer: True, False, True

Order of Operations (BODMAS / PEMDAS)

When an expression has multiple operations, you must follow a strict priority order to get the correct answer every time.

BODMAS RULE
B – Brackets
Innermost first: \((\;)\) then \([\;]\) then \(\{\;\}\)
O – Orders
Powers and roots: \(x^n,\; \sqrt{x}\)
D/M – ÷ ×
Left to right: Division and Multiplication
A/S – + −
Left to right: Addition and Subtraction
1
Evaluate using BODMAS
Calculate: \(\;3 + 6 \times (5 + 4) \div 3 - 7\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Bracket first: \(5+4=9\) → expression becomes \(3 + 6 \times 9 \div 3 - 7\)
2
Multiply: \(6 \times 9 = 54\) → \(3 + 54 \div 3 - 7\)
3
Divide: \(54 \div 3 = 18\) → \(3 + 18 - 7\)
4
Add/Subtract left to right: \(3+18=21\), then \(21-7=14\)
Answer: 14
2
Nested brackets & exponents
Evaluate: \(\;2^3 + \left[18 - (6 \times 2)\right] \div 2\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Innermost bracket: \(6 \times 2 = 12\) → \(2^3 + [18-12] \div 2\)
2
Square bracket: \(18-12=6\) → \(2^3 + 6 \div 2\)
3
Order (exponent): \(2^3=8\) → \(8 + 6 \div 2\)
4
Divide: \(6 \div 2 = 3\) → \(8+3\)
5
Add: \(8+3=11\)
Answer: 11

Fractions, Decimals & Percentages

Fractions, decimals and percentages are three representations of the same value. Fluency in converting between them and operating on them is essential for every branch of mathematics.

Fraction Operations

Adding and subtracting fractions requires a common denominator. Multiplying is straightforward; dividing requires flipping the second fraction (using the reciprocal).

Watch: Algebra – Fractions Review
FRACTION RULES
Add / Subtract
\(\dfrac{a}{b} \pm \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{ad \pm bc}{bd}\)
Multiply
\(\dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{ac}{bd}\)
Divide
\(\dfrac{a}{b} \div \dfrac{c}{d} = \dfrac{a}{b} \times \dfrac{d}{c} = \dfrac{ad}{bc}\)
Simplify
Divide numerator & denominator by HCF
1
Mixed fraction operations
Calculate: \(\;\dfrac{3}{4} + \dfrac{5}{6} - \dfrac{1}{3}\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
LCM of 4, 6, 3 = 12. Convert: \(\frac{3}{4}=\frac{9}{12},\quad\frac{5}{6}=\frac{10}{12},\quad\frac{1}{3}=\frac{4}{12}\)
2
\(\frac{9+10-4}{12} = \frac{15}{12}\)
3
Simplify: HCF(15,12)=3 → \(\frac{15}{12}=\frac{5}{4} = 1\frac{1}{4}\)
Answer: \(\dfrac{5}{4} = 1\dfrac{1}{4}\)
2
Division of fractions (real-world)
A ribbon of length \(\frac{7}{2}\) m is cut into pieces each \(\frac{7}{8}\) m long. How many pieces are obtained?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Number of pieces = Total length ÷ Piece length = \(\dfrac{7}{2} \div \dfrac{7}{8}\)
2
Flip and multiply: \(\dfrac{7}{2} \times \dfrac{8}{7} = \dfrac{56}{14} = 4\)
Answer: 4 pieces

Percentages & Percentage Change

Percentages express fractions out of 100. Percentage change is used everywhere — discounts, interest rates, growth rates, and statistics.

PERCENTAGE FORMULAS
Percentage of a Value
\(P\% \text{ of } X = \dfrac{P}{100} \times X\)
% Change
\(\dfrac{\text{New} - \text{Old}}{\text{Old}} \times 100\)
Fraction → %
\(\dfrac{a}{b} \times 100\)
% → Decimal
Divide by 100: \(35\% = 0.35\)
1
Discount problem
A laptop costs ₹60,000. It is on sale at 15% off. What is the sale price?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Discount = \(15\% \times 60000 = \frac{15}{100} \times 60000 = 9000\)
2
Sale Price = \(60000 - 9000 = 51000\)
Answer: ₹51,000
2
Percentage increase
A student scored 68 in a test last month and 85 this month. What is the percentage improvement?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
% Change = \(\dfrac{85-68}{68} \times 100 = \dfrac{17}{68} \times 100 = 25\%\)
Answer: 25% improvement
3
Reverse percentage
After a 20% increase, the price of a book is ₹360. What was the original price?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Let original price = \(P\). After 20% increase: \(P \times 1.20 = 360\)
2
\(P = \dfrac{360}{1.20} = 300\)
Answer: ₹300

Ratios compare quantities; proportions state that two ratios are equal. These concepts underpin everything from recipe scaling to financial analysis to physics.

Ratios & Equivalent Ratios

Watch: Algebra – Ratio & Proportion
RATIO RULES
Ratio

\(a:b = ka:kb\) for any \(k \neq 0\)
Divide in Ratio
Total \(\times \dfrac{\text{part}}{\text{sum of parts}}\)
Cross Multiplication
\(\dfrac{a}{b}=\dfrac{c}{d} \Rightarrow ad=bc\)
1
Sharing in a given ratio
Divide ₹2400 among A, B, and C in the ratio 3 : 4 : 5. Find each person's share.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Sum of ratio parts = \(3+4+5=12\)
2
A's share = \(\frac{3}{12}\times2400 = ₹600\)
3
B's share = \(\frac{4}{12}\times2400 = ₹800\)
4
C's share = \(\frac{5}{12}\times2400 = ₹1000\)
Answer: A = ₹600, B = ₹800, C = ₹1000
2
Direct & Inverse Proportion
(a) If 8 workers complete a job in 15 days, how many days will 12 workers take? (b) If a car travels 240 km in 4 hours, how far in 7 hours?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
a
More workers → fewer days (Inverse): \(8\times15 = 12\times d \Rightarrow d = \frac{120}{12} = 10\) days
b
More time → more distance (Direct): \(\frac{240}{4} = 60\) km/hr → \(7\times60 = 420\) km
Answer: (a) 10 days; (b) 420 km

Solving Linear Equations (One Variable)

Whatever you do to one side, do to the other
Verification

1
Multi-step linear equation
Solve for \(x\): \(\;\dfrac{2x+5}{3} - \dfrac{x-1}{2} = 4\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Multiply entire equation by LCM(3,2)=6: \(2(2x+5) - 3(x-1) = 24\)

2
Word problem → linear equation
Ravi is 3 times as old as his son. In 10 years, Ravi will be twice as old as his son. Find their current ages.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Let son's age = \(x\). Then Ravi's age = \(3x\).
2
In 10 years: \(3x+10 = 2(x+10)\)
3
Expand: \(3x+10 = 2x+20\) → \(x = 10\)
4
Son = 10, Ravi = 30. Check: In 10 years → 20 and 40 → 40=2×20 ✓
Answer: Son = 10 years, Ravi = 30 years

Simultaneous Equations

When two unknowns must be found simultaneously, we use systems of equations. These appear in circuit analysis, economics, geometry, and virtually every applied science.

Substitution Method

Watch: Systems of Equations – Video-Tutor.net
1
Substitution method
Solve: \(y = 2x - 1\) and \(3x + y = 14\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Substitute \(y=2x-1\) into equation 2: \(3x+(2x-1)=14\)
2
\(5x-1=14 \Rightarrow 5x=15 \Rightarrow x=3\)
3
Back-substitute: \(y=2(3)-1=5\)
Answer: x = 3, y = 5
2
Elimination method & word problem
A shop sells pens at ₹5 each and notebooks at ₹15 each. A customer buys 8 items totalling ₹80. How many of each did they buy?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Let pens = \(p\), notebooks = \(n\). \(p+n=8\) … (i), \(5p+15n=80\) … (ii)
2
Multiply (i) by 5: \(5p+5n=40\). Subtract from (ii): \(10n=40 \Rightarrow n=4\)
3
Substitute: \(p+4=8 \Rightarrow p=4\)
Answer: 4 pens and 4 notebooks

Exponents & Radicals (Surds)

Exponent laws are used in every advanced topic — exponential growth, scientific notation, logarithms, calculus power rule. Radicals (surds) are the inverse of exponents.

Laws of Exponents

Watch: Exponents – Video-Tutor.net
EXPONENT LAWS (a ≠ 0)
Product
\(a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}\)
Quotient
\(\dfrac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}\)
Power of Power
\((a^m)^n = a^{mn}\)
Zero Exponent
\(a^0 = 1\)
Negative Exponent
\(a^{-n} = \dfrac{1}{a^n}\)
Fractional Exponent
\(a^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{a^m}\)
Power of Product
\((ab)^n = a^n b^n\)
Power of Quotient
\(\left(\dfrac{a}{b}\right)^n = \dfrac{a^n}{b^n}\)
1
Simplify using exponent laws
Simplify: \(\;\dfrac{(x^3)^2 \cdot x^{-4}}{x^5}\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Power of power: \((x^3)^2 = x^6\) → numerator: \(x^6 \cdot x^{-4} = x^{6+(-4)} = x^2\)
2
Quotient: \(\dfrac{x^2}{x^5} = x^{2-5} = x^{-3}\)
3
Rewrite: \(x^{-3} = \dfrac{1}{x^3}\)
Answer: \(\dfrac{1}{x^3}\)
2
Simplifying radicals
Simplify: \(\sqrt{72} + 2\sqrt{8} - \sqrt{18}\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
\(\sqrt{72}=\sqrt{36\times2}=6\sqrt{2}\)
2
\(2\sqrt{8}=2\sqrt{4\times2}=2\times2\sqrt{2}=4\sqrt{2}\)
3
\(\sqrt{18}=\sqrt{9\times2}=3\sqrt{2}\)
4
Combine like terms: \(6\sqrt{2}+4\sqrt{2}-3\sqrt{2}=7\sqrt{2}\)
Answer: \(7\sqrt{2}\)

Factor a trinomial
Factor completely: \(6x^2 + 11x - 10\)

Quadratic Equations

Quadratics are degree-2 polynomials. Their solutions (roots) can be found by factoring, completing the square, or using the quadratic formula. The discriminant tells you the nature of roots without solving.

Quadratic Formula & Discriminant

Watch: Quadratic Equations – Video-Tutor.net
QUADRATIC FORMULAS
Standard Form
\(ax^2+bx+c=0\)
Quadratic Formula
\(x = \dfrac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}\)
Discriminant Δ
\(\Delta = b^2 - 4ac\)
Δ > 0
Two distinct real roots
Δ = 0
One repeated real root
Δ < 0
Two complex (imaginary) roots
Sum of roots
\(\alpha+\beta = -\dfrac{b}{a}\)
Product of roots
\(\alpha\beta = \dfrac{c}{a}\)
1
Solve using quadratic formula
Solve: \(2x^2 - 5x - 3 = 0\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
\(a=2, b=-5, c=-3\). \(\Delta = (-5)^2 - 4(2)(-3) = 25+24=49\)
2
\(x = \dfrac{5 \pm \sqrt{49}}{4} = \dfrac{5 \pm 7}{4}\)
3
\(x_1 = \dfrac{12}{4}=3\), \(x_2 = \dfrac{-2}{4}=-\dfrac{1}{2}\)
Answer: x = 3 or x = −1/2
2
Nature of roots & word problem
The length of a rectangle exceeds its width by 5 cm, and its area is 84 cm². Find the dimensions.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Let width = \(x\). Length = \(x+5\). Area: \(x(x+5)=84\)
2
\(x^2+5x-84=0\). \(\Delta=25+336=361=19^2\) → Two real roots
3
\(x=\frac{-5\pm19}{2}\): \(x=7\) or \(x=-12\) (reject negative)
4
Width=7 cm, Length=12 cm. Check: \(7\times12=84\;\checkmark\)
Answer: Width = 7 cm, Length = 12 cm

Statistics & Probability

Statistics helps you interpret and summarise data. Probability quantifies uncertainty. Together they form the language of data science, research, and decision-making.

Measures of Central Tendency & Dispersion

Watch: Central Tendency – Video-Tutor.net Watch: Intro to Statistics – Video-Tutor.net
STATISTICS FORMULAS
Mean
\(\bar{x} = \dfrac{\sum x_i}{n}\)
Median
Middle value when data is sorted (avg of two middles if n is even)
Mode
Most frequently occurring value
Range
Max − Min
Variance
\(\sigma^2=\dfrac{\sum(x_i-\bar{x})^2}{n}\)
Std Deviation
\(\sigma=\sqrt{\sigma^2}\)
1
Full statistical summary
For the data set: 4, 7, 13, 2, 7, 9, 4, 7 — find mean, median, mode, range, and standard deviation.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Sorted: 2, 4, 4, 7, 7, 7, 9, 13. \(n=8\)
2
Mean: \(\bar{x}=\frac{2+4+4+7+7+7+9+13}{8}=\frac{53}{8}=6.625\)
3
Median: Average of 4th & 5th values = \(\frac{7+7}{2}=7\)
4
Mode: 7 (appears 3 times)
5
Range: \(13-2=11\)
6
Variance: \(\sigma^2 = \frac{(2-6.625)^2+\ldots+(13-6.625)^2}{8} \approx 10.23\)
7
SD: \(\sigma \approx \sqrt{10.23} \approx 3.20\)
Mean=6.625, Median=7, Mode=7, Range=11, SD≈3.20

Basic Probability

Deck of cards probability
A card is drawn from a standard 52-card deck. Find: (a) P(Ace), (b) P(Red Card), (c) P(Ace OR Red Card).
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
a
\(P(\text{Ace})=\dfrac{4}{52}=\dfrac{1}{13}\)
b
\(P(\text{Red})=\dfrac{26}{52}=\dfrac{1}{2}\)
c
Aces that are Red = 2. \(P(A\cup R)=\frac{4}{52}+\frac{26}{52}-\frac{2}{52}=\frac{28}{52}=\frac{7}{13}\)
P(Ace)=1/13, P(Red)=1/2, P(Ace or Red)=7/13
2
Independent events
A fair die is rolled and a fair coin is tossed. What is the probability of getting a 5 on the die AND a Head on the coin?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Events are independent. \(P(5)=\frac{1}{6}\), \(P(H)=\frac{1}{2}\)
2
\(P(5 \text{ AND } H) = \frac{1}{6}\times\frac{1}{2}=\frac{1}{12}\)
Answer: 1/12
