Module 1

Functions & Pre-Calculus

Prerequisites: Algebra basics (linear equations, quadratics). Know what a variable is and how to solve for x.
Functions are the language of calculus. Before differentiating or integrating anything, you must be able to evaluate, compose, and invert functions, and understand exponential and logarithmic relationships.

Domain, Range & Function Notation

Watch: Review of Functions
FUNCTION ESSENTIALS
Function Notation
\(f(x)\) reads "f of x"; output for input x
Domain
All valid inputs (avoid ÷0 and √(negative))
Range
All possible outputs
Composition
\((f\circ g)(x)=f(g(x))\)
Inverse
Swap x↔y, solve for y: \(f^{-1}(x)\)
Verification
\(f(f^{-1}(x))=x\)
1
Domain, composition and inverse
Given \(f(x)=\sqrt{x-1}\) and \(g(x)=x^2+3\), find: (a) Domain of \(f\), (b) \((f\circ g)(x)\), (c) \(f^{-1}(x)\).
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
a
Need \(x-1\geq0\Rightarrow x\geq1\). Domain: \([1,\infty)\)
b
\(f(g(x))=f(x^2+3)=\sqrt{(x^2+3)-1}=\sqrt{x^2+2}\). Domain: all real x (x²+2≥2>0 always)
c
Set \(y=\sqrt{x-1}\). Square: \(y^2=x-1\Rightarrow x=y^2+1\). Swap: \(f^{-1}(x)=x^2+1\), domain \(x\geq0\)
Domain: [1,∞); (f∘g)(x)=√(x²+2); f⁻¹(x)=x²+1
2
Logarithm laws
Solve: \(\log_3(2x+1) = 3\) and simplify \(\ln(e^4) - \ln(e)\).
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Convert log form: \(3^3=2x+1\Rightarrow 27=2x+1\Rightarrow x=13\)
2
\(\ln(e^4)-\ln(e)=4\ln e - \ln e = 4-1=3\)
x = 13; \(\ln(e^4)-\ln(e)=3\)

Evaluating without a calculator

2
Proving a trig identity
Prove: \(\dfrac{\sin\theta}{1-\cos\theta} = \dfrac{1+\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Cross-multiply (to verify): LHS × sin θ = sin²θ; RHS × (1−cosθ) = (1+cosθ)(1−cosθ) = 1−cos²θ = sin²θ ✓
2
Formal proof from LHS: Multiply by \(\frac{1+\cos\theta}{1+\cos\theta}\): \(\dfrac{\sin\theta(1+\cos\theta)}{1-\cos^2\theta}=\dfrac{\sin\theta(1+\cos\theta)}{\sin^2\theta}=\dfrac{1+\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}\) = RHS ✓
Identity proved

Limits & Continuity

Prerequisites: Functions and function notation (Module 1). Understanding of what "approaches" means intuitively.
Limits are the rigorous foundation of calculus. The derivative and the integral are both defined using limits. Understanding limits prevents common errors in calculus courses.

Evaluating Limits

Watch: Limits – Video-Tutor.net
LIMIT LAWS & TECHNIQUES
Direct Substitution
If \(f(c)\) is defined, \(\lim_{x\to c}f(x)=f(c)\)
Factor & Cancel
For \(\frac{0}{0}\): factor numerator/denominator, cancel common factor
L'Hôpital's Rule
If \(\frac{0}{0}\) or \(\frac{\infty}{\infty}\): \(\lim\frac{f}{g}=\lim\frac{f'}{g'}\)
Squeeze Theorem
If \(g(x)\leq f(x)\leq h(x)\) and \(\lim g=\lim h=L\), then \(\lim f=L\)
Important Limit 1
\(\lim_{x\to0}\dfrac{\sin x}{x}=1\)
Important Limit 2
\(\lim_{x\to0}\dfrac{1-\cos x}{x}=0\)
1
Factor and cancel (0/0 form)
Evaluate: \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to3}\frac{x^2-9}{x-3}\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Direct sub: \(\frac{9-9}{3-3}=\frac{0}{0}\) → Indeterminate. Factor numerator.
2
\(\dfrac{(x+3)(x-3)}{x-3}=x+3\) (for \(x\neq3\))
3
\(\lim_{x\to3}(x+3)=6\)
Answer: 6
2
L'Hôpital's Rule
Evaluate: \(\displaystyle\lim_{x\to0}\frac{e^x - 1 - x}{x^2}\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Direct sub gives \(\frac{0}{0}\). Apply L'Hôpital: \(\lim_{x\to0}\frac{e^x-1}{2x}\)
2
Still \(\frac{0}{0}\). Apply again: \(\lim_{x\to0}\frac{e^x}{2}=\frac{e^0}{2}=\frac{1}{2}\)
Answer: 1/2
3
Continuity check
Is \(f(x)=\begin{cases}x^2-1 & x<2\\3 & x=2\\2x-1 & x>2\end{cases}\) continuous at \(x=2\)?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION (3 conditions for continuity)
1
\(f(2)=3\) ✓ (defined)
2
Left limit: \(\lim_{x\to2^-}(x^2-1)=3\). Right limit: \(\lim_{x\to2^+}(2x-1)=3\). So \(\lim_{x\to2}f(x)=3\) ✓
3
\(\lim_{x\to2}f(x)=3=f(2)\) ✓ All conditions met.
f is continuous at x = 2

sin limit
\(\lim_{x\to0}\frac{\sin x}{x}=1\)

Differentiation

Prerequisites: Limits (Module 3), function notation (Module 1), trig identities (Module 2).
The derivative measures instantaneous rate of change. It is the most-used operation in applied mathematics, physics, and engineering. You must master all differentiation rules.

Differentiate: \(y = x^3 \cdot e^{2x}\)

Find \(\dfrac{d}{dx}\left[\dfrac{\sin x}{x^2+1}\right]\)

Module 5

Applications of Derivatives

Prerequisites: Differentiation (Module 4). Ability to find and classify critical points.
Derivatives tell us where functions increase, decrease, and turn around. This is the heart of optimization — finding maximum profit, minimum cost, fastest speed — used in every engineering and business application.

Optimization & Critical Points

Watch: Applications of Derivatives
KEY TESTS
Critical Points
Set \(f'(x)=0\) or \(f'(x)\) undefined
1st Derivative Test
+→− at CP: local max; −→+ at CP: local min
2nd Derivative Test
\(f''(c)<0\): local max; \(f''(c)>0\): local min
Concave Up
\(f''(x)>0\)
Concave Down
\(f''(x)<0\)
Inflection Point
\(f''(x)=0\) and concavity changes
Mean Value Theorem
\(f'(c)=\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\) for some \(c\in(a,b)\)
1
Find maxima and minima
Find all local extrema of \(f(x)=x^3-6x^2+9x+2\) and classify them.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
\(f'(x)=3x^2-12x+9=3(x^2-4x+3)=3(x-1)(x-3)\)
2
Critical points: \(x=1\) and \(x=3\)
3
\(f''(x)=6x-12\). At \(x=1\): \(f''(1)=-6<0\) → Local Maximum. At \(x=3\): \(f''(3)=6>0\) → Local Minimum.
4
\(f(1)=1-6+9+2=6\). \(f(3)=27-54+27+2=2\)
Local Max at (1, 6); Local Min at (3, 2)
2
Real-world optimization
A farmer has 200 m of fencing to enclose a rectangular field. One side borders a river (no fence needed there). Find the dimensions that maximize the area.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Let width = \(x\), length = \(y\). Constraint: \(2x+y=200\Rightarrow y=200-2x\)
2
Area: \(A=xy=x(200-2x)=200x-2x^2\)
3
\(A'=200-4x=0\Rightarrow x=50\). \(A''=-4<0\) → Maximum.
4
\(y=200-100=100\). Area = \(50\times100=5000\) m²
Width = 50 m, Length = 100 m, Max Area = 5000 m²

\(f''(c)<0\)

\(f''(c)>0\)

Differential Equations

Prerequisites: Differentiation (Module 4) and integration (Module 6). Understanding of exponential and logarithmic functions.
A differential equation relates a function to its derivatives. They model population growth, cooling, electrical circuits, and mechanical vibrations. These appear in every engineering discipline.

Separable & Linear First-Order ODEs

Watch: Differential Equations
ODE METHODS
Separable ODE
\(\frac{dy}{dx}=f(x)g(y)\Rightarrow\int\frac{dy}{g(y)}=\int f(x)\,dx\)
Linear 1st Order
\(\frac{dy}{dx}+P(x)y=Q(x)\)
Integrating Factor
\(\mu(x)=e^{\int P(x)dx}\)
Solution
\(y=\dfrac{1}{\mu}\int\mu\cdot Q\,dx\)
Exponential Model
\(\frac{dy}{dt}=ky\Rightarrow y=Ce^{kt}\)
Newton's Cooling
\(\frac{dT}{dt}=k(T-T_{\text{env}})\)
1
Separable ODE
Solve: \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}=\dfrac{x}{y}\), given \(y(0)=3\).
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Separate: \(y\,dy=x\,dx\)
2
Integrate both sides: \(\dfrac{y^2}{2}=\dfrac{x^2}{2}+C\) → \(y^2=x^2+K\)
3
Apply IC \(y(0)=3\): \(9=0+K\Rightarrow K=9\)
4
\(y^2=x^2+9\Rightarrow y=\sqrt{x^2+9}\) (positive since y(0)=3>0)
\(y=\sqrt{x^2+9}\)
2
Exponential growth / decay
A bacteria culture starts with 500 cells and doubles every 3 hours. How many cells after 9 hours?
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Model: \(N=N_0\cdot2^{t/3}\). Initial \(N_0=500\).
2
At \(t=9\): \(N=500\cdot2^{9/3}=500\cdot2^3=500\times8=4000\)
4,000 cells after 9 hours
3
Linear first-order ODE
Solve: \(\dfrac{dy}{dx}+\dfrac{2}{x}y=x^2\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Integrating factor: \(\mu=e^{\int\frac{2}{x}dx}=e^{2\ln x}=x^2\)
2
Multiply: \(\frac{d}{dx}(x^2 y)=x^2\cdot x^2=x^4\)
3
Integrate: \(x^2 y=\dfrac{x^5}{5}+C\Rightarrow y=\dfrac{x^3}{5}+\dfrac{C}{x^2}\)
\(y=\dfrac{x^3}{5}+\dfrac{C}{x^2}\)

College Statistics

Prerequisites: Basic mean/median/mode from school statistics. Understanding of probability fundamentals.
College statistics extends into probability distributions, hypothesis testing, and confidence intervals — the statistical tools used in data science, research papers, and quality control.

Probability Distributions

DISTRIBUTIONS

\(\binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k}\)

\(\mu=np\)

\(\sigma^2=np(1-p)\)

1
Binomial distribution
A fair coin is tossed 10 times. Find: (a) P(exactly 6 heads), (b) Mean and standard deviation.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
a
\(n=10,\;p=0.5,\;k=6\). \(P(X=6)=\binom{10}{6}(0.5)^6(0.5)^4=210\times(0.5)^{10}=\dfrac{210}{1024}\approx0.205\)
b
Mean: \(\mu=10\times0.5=5\). SD: \(\sigma=\sqrt{10\times0.5\times0.5}=\sqrt{2.5}\approx1.58\)
P(X=6)≈0.205; Mean=5; SD≈1.58
2
Z-score and hypothesis testing
A factory claims its light bulbs last 1000 hours (σ=50). A sample of 25 bulbs gives \(\bar{x}=980\) hrs. Test at α=0.05 whether the true mean is less than 1000.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
H₀: μ=1000; H₁: μ<1000 (left-tailed)
2
\(Z=\dfrac{980-1000}{50/\sqrt{25}}=\dfrac{-20}{10}=-2.0\)
3
Critical value at α=0.05 (left tail): \(Z_{crit}=-1.645\)
4

Matrix multiplication & determinant
Given \(A=\begin{pmatrix}2&3\\1&4\end{pmatrix}\) and \(B=\begin{pmatrix}1&-1\\2&0\end{pmatrix}\), find \(AB\) and \(\det(A)\).
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1

2
\(\det(A)=2\times4-3\times1=8-3=5\)
\(AB=\begin{pmatrix}8&-2\\9&-1\end{pmatrix}\), det(A)=5
2
Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
Find the eigenvalues of \(A=\begin{pmatrix}4&1\\2&3\end{pmatrix}\) and the eigenvector for each.
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Characteristic equation: \(\det(A-\lambda I)=0\)
2
\(\det\begin{pmatrix}4-\lambda&1\\2&3-\lambda\end{pmatrix}=(4-\lambda)(3-\lambda)-2=\lambda^2-7\lambda+10=(\lambda-5)(\lambda-2)=0\)
3
Eigenvalues: \(\lambda_1=5,\;\lambda_2=2\)
4
For \(\lambda_1=5\): \((A-5I)\mathbf{v}=0\Rightarrow\begin{pmatrix}-1&1\\2&-2\end{pmatrix}\mathbf{v}=0\Rightarrow v_1=v_2\). Eigenvector: \(\begin{pmatrix}1\\1\end{pmatrix}\)
5
For \(\lambda_2=2\): \((A-2I)\mathbf{v}=0\Rightarrow\begin{pmatrix}2&1\\2&1\end{pmatrix}\mathbf{v}=0\Rightarrow 2v_1+v_2=0\). Eigenvector: \(\begin{pmatrix}1\\-2\end{pmatrix}\)
λ₁=5 with eigenvector [1,1]ᵀ; λ₂=2 with eigenvector [1,−2]ᵀ
3
Gaussian elimination
Solve the system using Gaussian elimination: \(x+y+z=6,\; 2x-y+z=3,\; x+2y-z=2\)
STEP-BY-STEP SOLUTION
1
Augmented matrix: \(\begin{pmatrix}1&1&1&|&6\\2&-1&1&|&3\\1&2&-1&|&2\end{pmatrix}\)
2
R2←R2−2R1; R3←R3−R1: \(\begin{pmatrix}1&1&1&|&6\\0&-3&-1&|&-9\\0&1&-2&|&-4\end{pmatrix}\)
3
R3←R3+R2/3: \(\begin{pmatrix}1&1&1&|&6\\0&-3&-1&|&-9\\0&0&-7/3&|&-7\end{pmatrix}\)
4
Back-substitute: \(z=3,\; -3y-3=-9\Rightarrow y=2,\; x+2+3=6\Rightarrow x=1\)
x = 1, y = 2, z = 3
